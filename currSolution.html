Adaptive strategies farmers can use to cope with the impacts of climate change

To keep farms strong against Fiji's changing weather, farmers are making smart choices to adapt their ways. Below are some strategies you can use

Crop diversification

In Fiji, crop diversification means planting a wider variety of crops instead of just one or two. This is a smart way to adapt to climate change because if drought, floods, or new pests damage one type of crop like sugarcane, you'll still have others like dalo, cassava, or vegetables to rely on, helping to secure food and income even when the weather is unpredictable.

Agroforestry

means planting trees alongside your food crops or where your animals graze. For Fiji, this is a smart way to adapt because the trees can provide shade for delicate crops and animals from the harsh sun, protect fields from strong winds during storms, and their roots help the soil hold more water during dry spells. It also makes the farm more diverse and resilient.

Drought-resistant and heat-tolerant crop varieties

Drought-resistant and heat-tolerant crop varieties are like special types of plants that are bred to handle tough conditions. For Fiji, this means using dalo, cassava, or vegetable seeds that can grow even when there's less rain than usual (drought-resistant) or when the sun is much hotter than before (heat-tolerant). By choosing these stronger varieties, farmers have a better chance of getting a good harvest even as our climate changes.

Improved soil management

Improved soil management means treating our farm soil with care, like using cover crops (planting special plants like beans or grasses when main crops aren't growing to protect the soil), mulching (putting dried leaves or grass on top of the soil), and adding organic amendments (like compost or animal manure). For Fiji, these methods make the soil like a super sponge, helping it hold more water during dry times and letting extra water drain away during heavy rains, ensuring our crops like dalo and vegetables stay healthy even with unpredictable weather.
